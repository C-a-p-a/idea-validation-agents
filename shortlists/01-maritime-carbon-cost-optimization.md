# Shortlist 01 — Maritime Carbon Cost Optimization

**Quick score:** 64/100 (TEST) · **Full validation:** `memory/ideas/nordic-maritime-emissions-compliance/decision_memo.md` · **NACE:** H50 (shipping)

## In plain words

Ships now have to pay for their CO₂ pollution under new EU rules, and the rules get stricter every year (100% from 2026). A medium shipping company (20 ships) ends up paying on the order of **~160 million NOK/year**. The rules are new, confusing, and expensive.

We build software that **manages and minimises that bill** for the shipping company — and handles the mandatory reporting. Think of it as a **budgeting / treasury tool for the carbon bill**, not a paperwork generator.

## What we actually solve (the value is NOT predicting the carbon price)

The carbon-permit price is a traded market — nobody predicts it reliably, so the product must NOT be "we tell you when to buy low." The real savings need no crystal ball:

1. **Visibility** — know exactly how many permits you'll need, ahead of time. Budget properly, stop panic-buying.
2. **Disciplined buying** — spread purchases instead of buying everything at the deadline (when everyone buys and the price spikes).
3. **Teaming up ships ("pooling") + who-pays-when-rented-out** — structural/contractual savings, zero dependence on the price.
4. **Burn less fuel** (speed/route) — fewer permits needed regardless of price.

Just 1–2% smarter ≈ **1.5–3 million NOK/year saved** for that one company → easy to justify paying for the tool.

## Why it scored well
- **Forced demand** — everyone must comply; you don't have to convince anyone they have the problem.
- **Big money** — millions per company → strong willingness to pay.
- **Real gap** — for mid-size operators, "help me minimise the cost" is underserved; competitors mostly say "don't get fined."

## The one big risk
Shipping is a closed, relationship-based industry. A pure software team struggles to land the first customers without an insider. **Founder asset:** 1 year on a coast-guard vessel — not commercial-shipping expertise, but real maritime exposure + a network seed, which makes finding a maritime co-founder/advisor realistic.

## Next step — Riskiest Assumption Test (≤4 weeks, ~$100)
1. Land **one credible maritime co-founder/advisor within 30 days** (via the team's network + Norwegian maritime cluster).
2. In parallel: **8 interviews** with mid-size Nordic operators — show a mock, probe the cost-minimisation pain.

**Pass:** insider secured + ≥5 of 8 confirm the gap is real + ≥2 want a paid pilot.
**Fail:** drop — don't build on hope (the lesson from the earlier seabed idea).
