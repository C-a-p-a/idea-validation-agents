# Shortlist — B2B SaaS candidates worth pursuing

Curated from the NACE-code idea sweep (2026-06-09). These are the ideas that survived scrutiny enough to warrant a real next step (a Riskiest-Assumption Test before building).

## The winning pattern ("the DNA")

Every idea here shares the same shape — the one that fit the founder team best:

> A **new EU/EEA regulation forces** businesses to do something → it **costs them real, recurring money** → software both (a) handles the mandatory compliance and (b) **reduces the cost** → B2B, sticky (mandatory = low churn), scalable EU-wide from Norway, buildable with AI, and **does NOT depend on predicting a volatile market.**

This is "**FinOps for a forced cost**" — manage and minimise a mandatory, expensive, ongoing obligation, the way cloud-FinOps tools manage cloud spend.

## Why CBAM is here (and why it's interesting)

CBAM is the **same DNA as the maritime idea** — a brand-new carbon cost, forced, expensive, recurring — but with **one big advantage: no closed-industry domain gap.** Importers and manufacturers are normal, reachable businesses (unlike the relationship-locked shipping world). Norway is also a major **exporter** of CBAM-covered goods (aluminium/Hydro, fertiliser/Yara, silicon/Elkem), whose EU customers now demand verified emissions data — a natural local wedge. It directly answers the maritime idea's weakness.

## Watchlist (similar DNA, not yet validated — flagged for honesty)

- **ETS2 — carbon cost on buildings & road fuel** (EU, from 2027): same carbon-cost shape, very large, but earlier-stage and buyer less defined. Worth tracking as the regulation nears.
- **EPR / packaging-fee optimization** (PPWR): producers pay packaging-waste fees; optimise reporting + minimise fees. Already in the sweep (`memory/ideas/epr-csrd-sustainability-reporting/`) — flagged as the most crowded category.
- **E-invoicing / digital tax-reporting mandates** (EU "ViDA", rolling out 2024–2030): every B2B company forced onto mandatory e-invoicing. Huge + forced, but it's pure compliance (little "save money" upside) and crowded (Pagero, Tungsten, Storecove).

## How to read a score

These use the idea-scoring multiplicative-floor model (0–100): **75+ pursue · 55–74 test · 35–54 pivot · <35 drop.** A "test" verdict means promising-but-unproven — run the Riskiest Assumption Test (a cheap, ≤2–4 week experiment) before writing real code. Full per-idea analysis lives in `memory/ideas/<slug>/decision_memo.md`.
